When you poke around your hard drive, you can see little thumbnail views of ICO (icon) files and CUR (cursor) files right by their filenames in Explorer. But what about BMP graphics--those bitmaps that you use for wallpaper or for expressing yourself in Microsoft Paint? Well, you can mess around in the Registry to make their Explorer icons into miniature "previews" of the graphics files. It's not even that hard to do:

    1. Make sure you have a backup of the Registry. Are you listening?
    2. Launch regedit by selecting Start/Run, typing regedit in the text box, and pressing Enter.
    3. Open HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT and the Paint.Picture (for Microsoft Paint, the name of the program that usually opens when you click BMP files).
    4. Look for the DefaultIcon entry, click the (Default) value, and change it to %1.

If this previewing business bugs you later on, select Start/Programs/Windows Explorer, and select View/Options from Explorer. Click the File Types tab, find the Bitmap Image file type in the list, click the Edit button, and click the Change Icon button. Browse to another icon file, and select it to eliminate the preview view in favor of the new icon. Click OK twice to close the dialog box.
